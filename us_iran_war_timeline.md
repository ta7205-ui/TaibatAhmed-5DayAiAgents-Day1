# Daily Timeline of the U.S.–Iran War & Energy Markets (June 14 – June 20, 2026)

This document provides a detailed daily timeline of the diplomatic and military events during the U.S.–Iran war in mid-June 2026, focusing on the opening and closure of the Strait of Hormuz, maritime oil flows, and corresponding daily energy prices.

---

## Executive Summary: Strait of Hormuz & Oil Flows

The Strait of Hormuz is the world's most critical energy chokepoint, carrying approximately 20% of global seaborne petroleum and liquefied natural gas (LNG). During the 110-day conflict (commencing February 28, 2026), Iran's blockade of the Strait and the subsequent U.S. counter-blockade of Iranian ports triggered a severe global energy crisis.

*   **Reopening (June 17):** Under the terms of the Versailles Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) signed on June 17, the Strait was declared open to toll-free commercial traffic. The immediate result was a collapse in the "war risk premium," causing Brent and WTI crude oil prices to plunge by roughly 7% in a single day.
*   **Re-closure Dispute (June 20):** Tensions in Lebanon between Israel and Hezbollah led Iran to declare the Strait closed again on June 20, claiming U.S. and Israeli bad faith. U.S. Central Command (CENTCOM) contested this, stating the Strait remains open and active under maritime surveillance. Prices reacted with a sharp pop of ~7% in weekend/early trading.
